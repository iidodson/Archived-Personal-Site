The User Testing Bug form is an internal tool for developers to report bugs to Quality Assurance (QA) engineers. I redesigned the form to decrease wordage and design inconsistencies, which would improve the overall quality of the data received.

Research

I interviewed QA engineers and developer to gain a deeper understanding of successes and failures of the current form.

My questions were geared towards understanding:

1. The reasoning behind the design and engineering decisions of the form
2. How the form integrated into their workflow
3. Which pieces of information were essential to their workflow vs which were not
4. The language they used to categorize bugs
5. The pain points they had when processing bugs

Findings

1. It took a lot of time to fill out the form because of the amount of textual information that was required to submit.
2. The instructions for some of the input field were not clear which made the user confused or unsure about what information the form was truly asking for.
3. The overall design of the form did not match the brand identity of the company

Objectives

Through conducting these user interviews, the objectives of the form became clear.

1. Reduce time it takes customers to fill in form
2. Clarify instructions for each input
3. Get concise data that is more useful to QA
4. Reduce the amount of textual information collected.
